Live a Life of Unending Optimism

July 18, 2026

2026   ·   personal     ·   life  

“How can I lose when I came here with nothing”

"Picture of Ajala Marvellous (27 M), wearing a white T-shirt with the inscription 'Omoluabi', a purple Yoruba traditional cap (fila) holding a blue polariod camera over one of his eyes and standing infront of a building with the inscription 'Museum'”

As I contemplated what to write this year, an interesting question that crossed my mind was “If I am to write to my 17 year old self, what would my present self say to him?” What do I know now that will be useful, insightful or anything to him then?

  • Live a life of unending Optimism: In the words of a friend I really admire, “Life is a pot of hot beans” and best believe in my little experience, it is even though I don’t think I can claim authority about life (well, going by the average life expectancy in Nigeria, I’m half already). The point is life is a very long time and it is filled with many things that frames it already in odds against you (forever you were born in Nigeria). So the best way to live is to have an unending optimism about life. That the universe is conspiring for your greatness, because it really is. As a spiritual person, my Bible tells me that “all things work together for the good of those called unto his purpose” and “eyes have not seen, neither has ears heard and it hasn’t crossed anyone’s mind yet, what God has in stock for me”. So you have to believe it with your very being, when the odds are stacked against you, when it doesn’t look like there is going to be a way, when you are at your wilt’s end, even then you have to believe that even at that, the universe is conspiring for your greatness. This is important because the alternative is believing that life is rigged against you, even if it is, you need to believe otherwise because nature has a way of accentuating whatever it is we believe. Remember that you can’t lose when you came here with nothing. This is also a good way to live to because in some ways, this is the least you’ll be. That is not to say there won’t be tough days ahead, but when there is life, there is plenty room for life to get better, so there is so much to he hopeful about.

“How can I lose when I came here with nothing?”

  • Be curious, question everything and thirst for knowledge: You have to stay curious about life and everything around you. Refuse to take any response on the surface level, question everything, ask why things work the way they work and things that don’t work also why they don’t. The world reveals itself to people who are patient enough to search diligently. Acknowledge that you know nothing and use that naivety to your advantage, desire to know things, deeply because that is your edge. Read, far and wide, for the fun of it, ask questions, desire to be an expert in many things. “For there is nothing new under the earth”, so the answer to whatever question you have is in some book, you just need to thirst enough for it.

  • Be a doer and do hard things: Your curiosity will open to you opportunities to change the world, you have to be brave and take the plunge. Avoid the path commonly taken, aim for things above and beyond what you think you can do, do hard things, do things you will typically avoid and that thing you will want to convince yourself to avoid. Most things won’t appear doable until you take the first step and it is in doing that you build capacity to become the person you were made to be and create the type of the world you desire to live. The world will not remember you for the volume of your knowledge, but by the world you contributed to building. Be a doer and do hard things for there lies your purpose

“As you become an adult, you realize that things around you weren’t just always there; people made them happen. But only recently have I started to internalize how much tenacity everything requires. That hotel, that park, that railway. The world is a museum of passion projects.” - John Collision

  • Work hard: There is no shortcut to life, you have to be willing to work hard, extremely hard till you know you have nothing else within you to give. Give everything your best, let people be able to attest to your hard work. Nothing will come easy, so you have to be willing and able to stick with things. But also know that hard work does not automatically translate to success, that you put in a lot of effort into something does not guarantee that it will automatically work out. But you should still try, give it your best and leave the rest to God

  • Lastly, live, love and be happy: Live in the moment, enjoy life, do things that excite you, do things that scare you, do things for fun, help strangers without expecting anything in return, be a fun person to be around, care about people, about friends, family. Love hard and truly, do not love in parts, not for the fear of what if it doesn’t work out, love with all your heart, give yourself truly away in the process, the love you give out has a way of finding its way back to you. Enjoy the mundane, do silly things that make you happy, hangout with friends, be someone they can always refer to and lastly, please, for the lack of better words, do not be an ASSHOLE.

In conclusion, I guess these words are not only relevant for my 17 year old self but also my present day self or maybe I am just forever 17 at heart. Happy birthday Marve, you are a star 🌟. Nations shall come to the brightness of your light💡
