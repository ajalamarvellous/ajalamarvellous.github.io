As I contemplated what to write this year, an interesting question that crossed my mind was "If I am to write to my 17 year old self, what would my present self say to him?" What do I know now that will be useful, insightful or anything to him then?

* *Live a life of unending Optimism:* In the words of a friend I really admire, "Life is a pot of hot beans" and best believe in my little experience, it is. Even though I don't think I can claim authority on that (though going by the average life expectancy in Nigeria, I'm half past already, so maybe I could 🤷🏽‍♂️🤷🏽‍♂️). The point is life is a very long time (if you don't die🌚) and it is filled with many lows that makes life feel it is at odds against you (for example you were born in Nigeria). So the best way to live is to have an unending optimism about life. That the universe is conspiring for your greatness, because it really is. According to the christian in me, my Bible tells me that "all things work together for the _my_ good..." and "eyes have not seen, neither has ears heard and it hasn't crossed anyone's mind yet, what God has in stock for me". So you have to believe it with your very being, when the odds are stacked against you, when it doesn't look like you're going to make it out alive, when you are at your wilt's end, even then is when you have to believe it the most, that the universe is conspiring for your greatness in that "low" you've found yourself. I think it is important you believe it because the alternative is believing that life is rigged against you, and trust me, sometimes, life feels so. However, nature has a way of accentuating whatever it is we believe to become our reality. Remember that you can't lose when you came here with nothing. I think another reason why it is important to have a live with unending optimism is because when you believe life is going to work in your favour, you are likely to see things through, stick with things for longer, primarily because you believe the story ends only when you win, but in reality you win because you stick to things, so in a way they both become as self perpetuating loop, you stick at for longer because you believe you will win, you win because you stick to things. You will have no incentive to stick to things if you have no believe things will get better, so live with an unending optimism. The final reason is more of pure delusion but it works nonetheless. In some ways, a good way to think about life is that, this is the least you'll be. That is not to say there won't be tough days ahead, but when you stick to things long enough and follow the other advice listed below, life generally swings upward in cycles and if you don't die, there is plenty opportunity ahead for life to get better, so there is just so much to he hopeful about.

>"Remember, how can you lose when you came here with nothing?"

* *Don't die:* As hilarious as this sounds, every other thing in this article is predicated on this one thing, that you make it out alive, so I need you to use every thing, arsenal and weapon at your disposal, PLEASE DON'T DIE. The journey ends the moment you do and you need to stay in the game long enough to win, so do whatever it takes to stay alive. Now, when I say don't die, it is not an invitation to become Methusalah. I mean everyone will still die, so it is not a question of cheating death, but till what you are here on earth to do, please don't die.

* *Be curious, question everything and thirst for knowledge*: You have to stay curious about life and everything around you. Refuse to take any response on the surface level, question everything, be interested in how the world works and why things work the way they work and things that don't work also why they don't. The world reveals itself to people who are patient enough to search diligently. Acknowledge that you know nothing and use that naivety to your advantage, desire to know things, deeply because that is your edge. Read, far and wide, for the fun of it, ask questions, desire to be an expert in many things. _"For there is nothing new under the earth"_, so the answer to whatever question you have is in some book, you just need to thirst enough for it.

* *Be a doer and do hard things:* Your curiosity will open to you opportunities to change the world, so you have to be brave and take the plunge. Avoid the path commonly taken, aim for things above and beyond what you think you can do, do hard things, do things you will typically avoid and that thing you will want to convince yourself to avoid. Most things won't appear doable until you take the first step and it is in doing that you build capacity to become the person you were made to be and create the type of the world you desire to live. The world will not remember you for the volume of your knowledge, but by the world you contributed to building. Be a doer and do hard things for there lies your purpose

> "As you become an adult, you realize that things around you weren't just always there; people made them happen. But only recently have I started to internalize how much tenacity everything requires. That hotel, that park, that railway. The world is a museum of passion projects." - John Collision

* *Work hard:* There is no shortcut to life, you have to be willing to work hard, extremely hard till you know you have nothing else within you to give. Give everything your best, let people be able to attest to your hard work. Nothing will come easy, so you have to be willing and able to stick with things. But also know that hard work does not automatically translate to success, that you put in a lot of effort into something does not guarantee that it will automatically work out. But you should still try, give it your best and leave the rest to God

* *Lastly, live, love and be happy:* Live in the moment, enjoy life, do things that excite you, do things that scare you, do things for fun, help strangers without expecting anything in return, be a fun person to be around, care about people, about friends, family. Love hard and truly, do not love in parts, you do not experience what love truly is without fully giving yourself away, so give yourself totally away in the process, love with all your heart, the love you give out has a way of finding its way back to you and if it per chance didn't work out, you will have no regret. Enjoy the mundane, do silly things that make you happy, hangout with friends, dance in the rain, wave to strangers, travel, aspire to see beyond your immediate environment, be someone people can rely on and lastly, please, for the lack of better words, do not be an ASSHOLE.

In conclusion, I guess these words are not only relevant for my 17 year old self but also my present day self or maybe I am just forever 17 at heart. Happy birthday Marve, you are a star 🌟. Nations shall come to the brightness of your light💡
